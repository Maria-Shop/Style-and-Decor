Design Styles: How Things Have Changed AND Stayed the Same

History

Like fashion, decorating trends always come back around again — here are a few concepts we found in our archives and how you can update them for today.

DIY Organizing:

1911

There was no IKEA, so homeowners turned to Good Housekeeping for do-it-yourself ideas, like this handy shoe organizer from our March 1911 issue.

DIY Organizing:

Now

We're addicted to the idea of decluttering. Entire stores, books and websites are dedicated to helping us organize our stuff.

Graphic Prints:

1951

The rule for pattern was pick one and use it throughout a room, on everything from curtains to chair covers.

Graphic Prints:

Now

Pattern play is the name of the game. Mix two, three or even four in one space, from geometric to floral to tribal.

Walls That Wow:

1961

The counterculture carried over into decor, with saturated colors reigning supreme. Think high-impact walls, bright curtains and crazy patterns.

Walls That Wow:

Now

Less is more. Use classic white wainscoting on the bottom portion of a wall to keep bold paper from overwhelming a room.
