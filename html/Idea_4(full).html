Style Tricks for a Bedroom Your Kids Won't Outgrow

Bedroom

It may feel like you — and your wallet — can't keep up with your child's ever-evolving interests. Here's how to design a room that will suit her style (and needs) until she's ready to leave the nest. No Frozen-themed furniture, promise!

When You've Got... A Baby

Yes, giving a room a fresh coat of paint is cheap and easy, but come on, no one wants to do that every year. A soothing backdrop for a contemporary crib, this gender-neutral blue allows bedding and toys to bring added pops of color and will last well into your kids' teen years.

When You've Got... A Toddler

If you're looking for furniture with legs, skip the toddler bed and go straight for the grown-up one. Teamed with a bold patterned quilt and a vibrant lampshade and pillows, this classic iron bed will be an ageless backdrop.

When You've Got... A Teen

Fact: Teenagers love their stuff, and they'll spend more time than you'd expect holed up in their rooms with it. That means multiple storage solutions are key. Closet organizers and bins keep clothing and accessories orderly and accessible. Here, a more permanent flip-top window seat lets your kid seamlessly stash bulky stuff, like extra blankets and those stuffed animals she's not ready to part with.
